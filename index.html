Extreme weather events are hitting the United States every three days.1 This interactive map presents the intersection of FEMA's federally declared climate disasters, awarded at the county level (2011 to 2023) with Congressional Districts. Read more about how communities are experiencing climate change in the Atlas of Disaster by Rebuild by Design.

How To Use

1) Explore Disasters: Simply navigate across the map to view the counties affected by federally declared disasters, or add a specific address.

2) Reveal County Information: Click on a county to see the specific number of disasters it has experienced and to access the information of the corresponding congressional district representative and state senator.

3) Take Action: Armed with this information, we encourage you to reach out to your representatives. Share your insights from the map and urge them to prioritize resilient infrastructure projects that safeguard our communities.

Methodology

This map is built on a foundation of research and data sources provided by U.S. Government:

  • FEMA Data: Disaster declarations for climate events occurring 2011-2023, courtesy iParametric
  • County Districts: Geographic boundaries from ESRI (2020)
  • Congressional Districts: Geographic boundaries from USDOT BTS
  • Current Legislature Database: Representative contact information from US Github Repository
